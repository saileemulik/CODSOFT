Kalpana Chawla was born on March 17, 1962 in Karnal . She was being raised in a society where boys are favored, and girls expected to be obedient , so she decided to beat the odds and follow her dreams with the support of her mother.

In order to fulfill her desire of becoming an astronaut, she aimed at joining the NASA and moved to the United States in 1982 . Kalpana obtained a Master’s degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington in 1984.

Kalpana liked poetry, dancing, cycling, and running. She used to take part in various sports events and was invariably first in all races. She has also played badminton and dodgeball with boys.

To become an astronaut Kalpana decided to have higher education. In 1986, she attained another master degree. In 1988, at Boulder, from the University of Colorado , she completed a Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering .

Her first flight was STS-87 , the fourth U.S Microgravity Payload flight , on Space Shuttle Columbia from Nov. 19 to Dec. 5, 1997.

NASA selected her as an astronaut candidate in December 1994 , and with her diligent work, soon, she became a mission specialist and operated Columbia’s robot arm .

She was certified commercial pilot for seaplanes, multi-engine aircraft, and glider, along with also certified flight Instructor for glider and airplanes.

For her excellent services, she achieved many awards such as the Congressional Space Medal of Honor, NASA Space Flight Medal, and NASA Distinguished Service Medal.

On 1st February, 2003 , she died in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster,along with 7 crew members, which happened when the Space Shuttle disintegrated over Texas during re-entry into the Earth’s atmosphere.
